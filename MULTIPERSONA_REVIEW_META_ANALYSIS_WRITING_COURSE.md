## QURANIC REFRAINS INVENTORY (20 Closings)

1. "And the company knew, and the regulators knew, but the published papers did not tell—and billions were spent on a half-truth."
2. "PRISMA is the covenant between author and reader: I will show you everything—how I searched, what I found, what I excluded, why."
3. "Write the protocol before you see the data. Lock it in a public registry. Then follow it—or explain why you deviated."
4. "The title is your first word to the reader. Make it complete. Make it honest."
5. "Do not spin. Do not hide. If the primary outcome was null, say so."
6. "Define your question with precision. PICO is the map before the journey."
7. "Search wide. Search deep. The evidence you miss may be the evidence that matters most."
8. "Every exclusion must have a reason. Every reason must be documented."
9. "A meta-analysis of biased studies yields a biased conclusion—with a narrower confidence interval. You have made the lie more precise."
10. "Extract in duplicate. Check each number. One digit wrong can change the conclusion."
11. "Choose the measure that fits the data. The wrong measure pools apples with oranges."
12. "Do not pool for the sake of pooling. A meta-analysis of incompatible studies is not synthesis—it is confusion."
13. "I-squared is not just a number to report. It is a question: Why do these studies disagree?"
14. "The file drawer is not empty. It holds the studies that companies hid, the results that journals rejected."
15. "The forest plot hides nothing. Every study visible. Every weight transparent."
16. "The effect size is the what. GRADE certainty is the how sure."
17. "The Discussion is not for advocacy. It is for honest interpretation."
18. "Transparency is not optional. Declare your funding. Declare your conflicts."
19. "You have gathered the evidence. You have weighed it fairly. You have written it transparently."
20. "Write so that truth may be found, and found again, by those who follow."
